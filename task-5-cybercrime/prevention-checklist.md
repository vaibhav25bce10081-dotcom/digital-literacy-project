# 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist

1. **The Golden UPI Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. If someone asks for your PIN to "verify" an incoming payment, it is a scam.
2. **Verify Recipient Names:** Always double-check the recipient's name displayed in the UPI app before hitting 'Pay.' Scammers often use names similar to official entities.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on all email accounts and social media profiles to add an extra layer of security beyond just a password.
4. **Use Unique Passwords:** Use a password manager to ensure every academic and personal account has a unique, complex password.
5. **Inspect Links (Phishing Awareness):** Hover over links in emails or SMS to check the actual URL. Look for subtle misspellings (e.g., "g00gle.com" instead of "google.com").
6. **Public Wi-Fi Hygiene:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or sensitive student portals while connected to public or campus Wi-Fi without a VPN.
7. **Social Media Privacy:** Restrict your profile visibility. Do not post photos of your ID cards, hall tickets, or internship offer letters which contain PII (Personally Identifiable Information).
8. **Keep Software Updated:** Regularly update your phone's OS and banking apps to ensure you have the latest security patches.

## Reporting Cybercrime
If you are a victim of cyber fraud:
- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call immediately within the "Golden Hour" of the fraud).
