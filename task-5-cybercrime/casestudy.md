# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud (The "Request Money" Scam)

## What is the Crime?
UPI Fraud involves deceiving individuals into authorizing unauthorized transfers from their bank accounts using Unified Payments Interface apps. A particularly common variant is the "Request Money" or "QR Code" scam, where victims are tricked into thinking they are receiving funds when they are actually sending them.

## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. **The Bait:** A scammer posts a fake advertisement on social media or platforms like OLX, offering a high-value item (like a laptop or smartphone) at an unbelievably low price.
2. **The Contact:** When a victim (often a student looking for a deal) expresses interest, the scammer builds trust through professional communication.
3. **The Trap:** The scammer claims they cannot meet in person and offers to pay or receive a deposit via UPI. They send a "Collect Request" or a QR code through WhatsApp or a payment app.
4. **The Deception:** The scammer tells the victim, "Just scan this code and enter your PIN to receive the payment."
5. **The Theft:** Because many users do not realize that a PIN is only required to *send* money, the victim enters their credentials. The money is instantly debited from their account and moved through a series of "mule" accounts.

## Who is Targeted?
While anyone can be a target, college students and young professionals are frequently targeted due to their high volume of digital transactions and frequent use of secondary marketplaces.

## Consequences
Beyond the immediate financial loss—which can range from a few thousand to several lakhs of rupees—victims often face significant psychological stress, a sense of violation, and a loss of confidence in digital banking systems. Recovering funds is notoriously difficult once the transaction is authorized by the user.
